I love to create.
Growing up, I would spend my time after school composing music. I didn’t know my love of creating could be found in anything outside of music until I moved out to the Bay Area after my freshman year of college. I was doing the marketing for a startup and took a leave of absence from college to continue working with them. We went through the third batch of 500 Startups and it was through this experience of working around other teams that I realized I wanted to be on the creation end.
When I returned to college the following semester, I started teaching myself how to program. Since I didn’t have a project to work on, I needed another source of motivation. I volunteered to teach a programming class for beginners and used the date of the class as my deadline to get down the basics. Through designing the posters for this class, I found an even greater love—design.
Looking back on my childhood, this passion makes complete sense. As a child, I was always finding ways to make common things more beautiful. I would paint rocks with nail polish and use my mother’s lipstick to decorate the toilet paper. ( Or maybe I was just a weird child. ) Since then, I’ve matured a tiny bit and now see beauty in the experience as well.
Nothing makes me happier than creating beautiful experiences and working with a talented team who believes in their product.
